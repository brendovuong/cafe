Revenue inputs
Daily customers How many individual people walk through your door in a day. A small specialty cafe typically sees 60–150/day. Higher foot traffic = more revenue but also more staff pressure. ? 80
Avg ticket Average amount one customer spends per visit. Calculated as total daily revenue ÷ number of customers. A drip coffee = ~$4 ticket. Pour-over + pastry = ~$13 ticket. Your bean and menu choices directly shift this number. ? $7.50
Days open / month How many days per month you operate. Most cafes run 26–28 days (closed 2–4 days). Fewer days = lower revenue but also lower labor costs. Factor in holidays and your own rest days. ? 26
Cost inputs
Monthly rent Your lease payment for the physical space. Typically the single largest fixed cost. Industry rule of thumb: rent should be no more than 10–15% of monthly revenue. Oakland/Bay Area ranges from ~$3,500–$8,000/mo for a small cafe. ? $4,500
Staff count Number of people on payroll. This model assumes ~$1,800/mo base wage + $350/mo in taxes and benefits per person. As owner-barista you likely count yourself here. A 3-person team covers ~2 shifts with some overlap on busy mornings. ? 3
COGS % Cost of Goods Sold — the direct cost of everything in the cup: beans, milk, syrups, cups, lids, sleeves, napkins, food ingredients. Does NOT include rent, wages, or utilities. Specialty cafes typically run 28–35%. Single-origin Ethiopian beans push this toward 32–35% unless your pricing reflects the premium. ? 32%
